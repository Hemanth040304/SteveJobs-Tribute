Steve Jobs

1955 - 2011

Design is not just what it looks like and feels like. Design is how it works.

Biography

Steven Paul "Steve" Jobs was an American information technology entrepreneur and inventor. He was the cofounder, chairman, and CEO of Apple Inc; CEO and largest shareholder of Pixar Animation Studios; and founder, chairman, and CEO of NeXT Inc. Jobs is widely recognized as a pioneer of the microcomputer revolution of the 1970s, along with Apple co-founder Steve Wozniak.

Creative entrepreneur whose passion for perfection and ferocious drive revolutionized six industries: personal computers, animated movies, music, phones, tablet computing, and digital publishing.

Life Timeline

1955

Born in San Francisco

Steven Paul Jobs was born and later adopted by Paul and Clara Jobs.

1960

Moving to Silicon Valley

Moved with his family to Mountain View, in the heart of what became Silicon Valley.

1969

Meeting Wozniak

Met Steve Wozniak through a mutual friend and bonded over electronics and pranks.

1972

Blue Box Creation

Built and sold illegal blue boxes with Wozniak that enabled free phone calls.

1976

Apple I Demo & Apple Founded

Showed the Apple I board at the Homebrew Computer Club and soon after co-founded Apple Computer Inc. with Steve Wozniak and Ron Wayne.

1978

Apple II Launch

Released the Apple II, the first mass-market personal computer.

1983

Lisa Computer

Launched the Lisa computer, one of the first with a graphical user interface, but it failed commercially.

1984

Macintosh Launch

Introduced the Macintosh, the first successful mass-market computer with a GUI and mouse.

1985

Departure from Apple

Resigned from Apple after a power struggle and founded NeXT.

1992

NeXTSTEP Licensed

Licensed NeXTSTEP operating system to run on Intel-based computers.

1996

Return to Apple

Returned to Apple after it acquired NeXT for $400 million.

1997

Becomes Interim CEO

Became interim CEO of Apple and brought in his NeXT executive team.

1998

iMac

Unveiled the iMac, a colorful, all-in-one computer that revived Apple’s design identity and sales.

2001

iPod

Introduced the iPod, a portable music player that revolutionized how people listened to music.

2003

iTunes

Launched the iTunes Store, enabling users to legally buy and download digital music.

2006

MacBook

Released the first MacBook, replacing iBook and PowerBook, marking Apple’s shift to Intel processors.

2007

iPhone

Revealed the iPhone, combining phone, music player, and internet in one device, transforming the smartphone industry.

2008

MacBook Air

Announced the MacBook Air, the thinnest laptop of its time, redefining portable computing.

2010

iPad

Launched the iPad, creating a new category between smartphones and laptops.

2011

Resignation as CEO

Resigned as CEO due to health reasons; Tim Cook took over leadership.

2011

Death

Died peacefully at home, surrounded by his family.

Steve Jobs' vision continues to inspire innovation worldwide.
